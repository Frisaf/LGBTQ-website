Most common gender identities

A gender identity is a person's individual experience of gender. Gender identity and assigned gender (birth sex) at birth are not the same things. Someone can identify as the same gender they were assigned at birth though. That is called cisgender. If you don't identify with the gender you were assigned at birth you are, per definition, transgender.

There are several different so called umbrellas underneath the transgender umbrella. This includes, but is not limited to the nonbinary umbrella and the binary umbrella. They, in turn, have other identities and umbrellas underneath themselves.


A list of the most common gender identities

  • Cisgender - A cisgender person is a person who identifies with their assigned gender at birth.
