Most common sexual orientations

"Sexual orientation - a person's identity in relation to the gender or genders to which they are sexually attracted; the fact of being heterosexual, homosexual, etc." (Google)

Not to be confused with romantic attraction. The difference between sexual and romantic attraction is that sexual attraction is who you'd want to have "the dirty" with while romantic attraction is attraction that makes people desire romantic contact or interaction with someone else.

Please note that sexual orientation is way more complicated that black and white. There are several different labels, because everyone feels different. One could, for example, be bisexual but not feel any romantic attraction. Some may not want to label their sexual orientation either, and therefore are unlabeled.

A list of the most common sexual orientations

  • Heterosexual - Sexual attraction to people of a different gender than their own.

  • Homosexual - Sexual attraction to people of the same gender as their own. This may refer to both gay and lesbian people.

  • Bisexual - Sexual attraction to two or more genders.

  • Pansexual - Sexual attraction to all genders, regardless of gender.

  • Asexual and the asexual spectrum - People who feel little to no sexual attraction. This is a broad spectrum which also includes demisexual, gray-asexual among with several other sexual identities.

  • Unlabeled - A term used by individuals who do not wish to label their identity with more specific terms such as bisexual, xenogender etc.
